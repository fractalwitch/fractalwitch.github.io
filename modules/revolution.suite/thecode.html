FAG BALLS EXPOSED

a real 1932 tabloid headline about the Hamilton Lodge Ball, recreated typographically — the design object the manifest already called for, not a scan of the original page.

1869 · Hamilton Lodge Ball, Harlem — the first drag ball in U.S. history. By its 1920s peak, up to 8,000 dancers and spectators.

1919 · Magnus Hirschfeld opens the Institut für Sexualwissenschaft, Berlin — the world's first research institute of its kind.

1928 · Ma Rainey's "Prove It On Me Blues" — a record label sells queerness as scandal, and Rainey uses their own ad copy to say it out loud.

May 6 1933 · Nazi students burn Hirschfeld's library — roughly 10,000 books, most of them irreplaceable.

1952 · Gladys Bentley, "I Am Woman Again" — the Lavender Scare's shape, in one woman's own words.
